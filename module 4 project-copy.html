Brief History

Chopin's Early Life

Frederic Chopin was born on March 1, 1810 in Poland. He started to play the piano at age 6,
which was fitting for him because his artistic inclination had always been apparent even from
a young age.

Chopin made an appearance at a charity concer, which was his first ever at age 8. Only three
years later, he performed for the Russian tsar Alexander I. His notoriety only increased by the
day as a child prodigy.

Early Works

His first composition was Polonaise in G Minor, and it was first played in a parade for a grand
duke's military band. He went on to create other Polonaises, mazurkas, variation, and ecossaises.
He went on to study music at the Warsaw Conservatory of Music at age 16.


Chopin's Most Notable Works

  • Ballade No. 1 in G-Minor Op 23
  • Fantaisie-Impromptu
  • Nocturn No. 20 in C-Sharp Minor

Additional Info

Fun fact about Chopin's Fantaisie-Impromptu piece;
he requested that it would never be released posthumously.
Can you imagine all the pieces we would not have if his
wishes were entirely respected?
